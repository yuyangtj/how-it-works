After visiting the Falun copper mine (Falun Gruva) in Sweden, I found the hook hoist — Christopher Polhem's "Hakspelet" from around 1700 — fascinating, and I wanted to understand how it works by building a 3D model. I don't know exactly how the original worked: this model is my and the LLMs' best guess, based on the description and the model displayed in the museum.

Fun fact: the famous outdoor moving staircase of Lustiga Huset at Gröna Lund (Stockholm) works on the same principle — two sets of steps rise and fall in counter-phase, and you step from one set to the other at the top of each stroke, just like the barrel handed between the hoist's two beam pairs.
