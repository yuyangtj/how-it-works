Hakspelet — Polhem's Hook Hoist

Falun copper mine, c. 1700. Water from the dam pond runs down the flume onto the water wheel; a crank and flatrod (stånggång) carry its push-pull to a rocking beam (vippbom) on the headframe; from its opposite arms hang two pairs of hooked timber beams on chains, counterbalancing each other. When one pair rises the other falls, and the barrel is handed from hook to hook — no ropes at all.

The cutaway shows the mine shaft: barrels are hooked on in the loading gallery below ground and climb the shaft one hook-spacing per half-stroke — up to the hoist-house floor, just above the machinery.
